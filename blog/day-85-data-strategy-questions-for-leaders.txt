14:T865,<p>A gentle ask to the data leaders in my network üëã</p>
<p>I'm now 85 days into my role as Head of Data &#x26; Cloud, and I'm deep in the exciting (and daunting) challenge of shaping our integrated data and cloud strategy from the ground up.</p>
<p>This isn't just about picking tools or building a lot of dashboards; it's about aligning every data decision with the company's core mission. Inspired by the post about data strategy from Shachar Meir, a good data strategy should support business goals and purpose.</p>
<p>The following pillars should be included. I wonder did I miss something:</p>
<h2>The Six Pillars</h2>
<ol>
<li>
<p><strong>Data Governance:</strong> Standards, quality, ownership, and regulatory compliance.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Data Architecture:</strong> Defining how data flows, where it lives, and how it's modeled.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Data Infrastructure:</strong> Moving towards a Hybrid Model (on-premise and cloud) for flexibility and scale.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Data Literacy &#x26; Culture:</strong> Ensuring the organization can actually use the data effectively.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>People &#x26; Process:</strong> Our operating model and crucial 'make vs. buy' decisions.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Success KPIs:</strong> Defining what 'winning' looks like.</p>
</li>
</ol>
<p>Here's my three questions for those of you who have successfully built and implemented a unified Data &#x26; Cloud Strategy:</p>
<h2>ü§î Question 1</h2>
<p>Should data and cloud strategy be defined hands-in-hand or separately?</p>
<h2>ü§î Question 2</h2>
<p>Like Cloud/IT Security be its own top-level pillar, or is it purely a component of Governance/Infrastructure? Did I miss any pillar?</p>
<h2>ü§î Question 3</h2>
<p>Who should be involved in drafting this data strategy?</p>
<p>Thank you in advance for your insight! üëá</p>
